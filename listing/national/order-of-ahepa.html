Order of AHEPA

Advancing Hellenism through service, education, and community since 1922.

Directions
The Order of AHEPA (American Hellenic Educational Progressive Association) is the largest and oldest Greek-American fraternal organization, founded in 1922 in Atlanta, Georgia. Established to combat discrimination against Greek immigrants and help them become engaged members of American society, AHEPA has grown into an international organization dedicated to promoting Hellenism, education, philanthropy, civic responsibility, and community service. Inspired by the ideals of ancient Greece, the Order continues to unite members through cultural, educational, charitable, and leadership initiatives across the globe.

Today, AHEPA has initiated more than 500,000 members throughout its history and maintains over 400 active chapters in 11 countries across three continents. The organization awards more than $1.8 million in scholarships annually, provides humanitarian assistance worldwide, advocates on issues important to the Hellenic community, and supports educational and cultural programs that strengthen ties between Greece, Cyprus, and the diaspora. Headquartered in Washington, D.C., AHEPA continues its century-long mission of fostering Hellenic ideals, encouraging volunteerism, developing future leaders, and serving communities through philanthropy, advocacy, and public service.
